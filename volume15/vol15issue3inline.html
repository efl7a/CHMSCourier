A Message from the Principal

Dear families,


The first week of school is now under our belts and, aside from computer outages and broken water pipes, it has been a great success!! We apologize for the late buses but this is always expected during the first week or two as the district adjusts routes to meet the needs of unexpected ridership. Thank you for your patience!

As of right now our numbers are down a bit from what was originally enrolled. We seem to be around 1920 as opposed to 1980. We will continue to assess our No-Shows to see if there is any availability in elective courses where some of you have made requests. Often times some kids show up the day after Labor Day so we do plan to wait until next week to make changes.

Monday is a school holiday for everyone. Don't forget that our 6th grade dance is Friday, September 6th from 4:15pm - 6:00pm. Your child should bring home information about this event so ask if you have not seen it. Also, mark September 12th on your calendars if you are interested in our Navigating Middle School Social Media event. This will begin at 6pm in the school cafeteria and it is for parents only.

All 7th grade students must have their current immunization records submitted by the 30th day of school in order to remain enrolled. If you are not certain if we have yours, please email our nurse at stefani1.abbate@cms.k12.nc.us.

Finally, if your 7th or 8th grader is planning to try out for a fall sport, please make sure you have checked the athletics webpage and submitted ALL documents electronically along with your current physical...all of this is required in order to participate in tryouts.

Enjoy your holiday and we will see everyone on Tuesday!!

Future Problem Solving Club

After last year’s success at State Levels+, Future Problem Solving will continue at CHMS! FPS is a club where students are taught to think creatively about the future. This will be conducted every Thursday after school from 4.30 pm to 5.30 pm. Students research a topic to gather background information, become mini-experts in the topic, and are taught to predict possibilities for the future. Up to five topics are researched over the academic year and can be used as a curriculum, integrated into other content areas, or offered as an extracurricular option. It involves different topics in science and technology, business and economics, and social and political issues. Recent topics that FPS offered were nanotechnology, e-commerce, rage and bullying, global interdependence, invasive species, climate change/threat, space junk, and pandemic. This year the topics included are International Travel, Sleep Patterns, Gamification, and Living in Poverty.


If you want to be a mini-scientist predicting problems and suggesting solutions to the future problems that could arise in the community or the world, please join this club. We need more creative and logical minds to make our future even brighter!


For further details please attend the information session on September 12 at 5 pm at CHMS in the art room. Hope to see you there!
