How to Help Your Child at Home

We are going to begin a new segment in our Courier newsletter designed to provide parents helpful tips for assisting their middle school child at home. If you have specific questions that you would like tips for, please email Ms. Brooks so that she can address your needs in future newsletters. This week‚Äôs first tip:

Create a homework station somewhere in your house. Stock the area with pens, pencils, paper and a calculator. When your child sits down to complete homework, check in once and ask him/her to pull up his/her Powerschool account for you. Have your child review the class grades with you and tell you what needs to be completed. This will help your child take ownership of grades. You do not have to have your own Powerschool account to keep track of your child‚Äôs progress. However, if you want one and do not have one set up yet, email Kelly Hoagland and she can help you set up your own account.
