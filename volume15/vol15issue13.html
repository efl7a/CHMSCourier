A Message from Principal Brooks

Dear families,

We have successfully completed the first semester…yay!! Monday will be the first day of second semester and students will rotate to their new elective course(s). I need to remind families of the procedures for schedule change requests. Although most of our courses are at capacity, if you would like to request a change in the event there is space, please email your grade level counselor. Changes are infrequent due to capacity issues, but if a change can be accommodated we will let you know.

Beginning a new semester is a perfect time to talk with your students about their academic responsibilities. Do you have procedures at home for homework time? Do you have a set location for completing homework? Does your child have goals for the new grading period? Are there rules for cell phones during homework time? These are all good things to have in place to make your evening and your child’s evening go more smoothly. Additionally, have a set day of the week that you and your child check Power School together. This helps you both stay on top of assignments and progress. It is not a good idea to check every day as this can lead to anxiety…for you and your child. Start off the new semester with some positive procedures designed to make the whole household’s routine more stress-free ☺

Thank you for all you do to support your children and our school. Have a great week!

National Junior Honor Society

Induction Process

Each spring, 7th grade students are inducted into NJHS and participate in the club for the remainder of 7th grade year and the following 8th grade year. All 7th grade students will be invited to have their grades reviewed by the 7th grade counselor to determine eligibility for application. This invitation will be extended after the first semester of 7th grade ends. Invitations typically go out late February. Once a student returns the invitation, grades will be reviewed. In order to receive an application, a student has to have a final 6th grade report card of "A's" with no more than 2 "B's" and 7th grade, 1st semester grades of "A's" with no more than 2 "B's." Students who meet this eligibility requirement will be invited to apply and must complete an application. The application will include an essay, teacher recommendation regarding character and leadership, and documentation of community service. Select staff review the application and use a rubric to determine if membership will be offered.
